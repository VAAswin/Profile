Case Study

Featured Project

AI Proposal Bot — From 5 Days to 1 Day

How I used Claude + Gemini to eliminate a pre-sales bottleneck at Petrus Technologies

The Problem

Every enterprise proposal at Petrus took 4–5 days to draft. Sales engineers were spending most of their time formatting documents instead of selling. We were losing deal momentum because prospects waited too long for proposals after demos.

What I Discovered

Through interviews with the sales team and review of 5 lost deals, I found 3 root causes:

  1. No reusable content library — every proposal started from scratch
  2. Technical specs had to be manually pulled from 5 different internal docs
  3. No clear owner — proposals bounced between sales, pre-sales, and engineering

What I Built

I defined the requirements for an AI-powered Proposal Bot using Claude (Anthropic) and Gemini. The bot:

  • Ingested our product documentation and past proposals as context
  • Generated a first draft proposal in under 2 hours from a structured intake form filled by the sales rep
  • Used prompt engineering to maintain our tone, technical accuracy, and pricing structure
  • Required only 30 minutes of human review and customization before sending

Why These Decisions

  • Claude was chosen for long-context document understanding; Gemini for structured data extraction.
  • Intake form was the key UX insight — it forced sales reps to clarify scope before generation, improving draft quality by 60%.
  • I kept a human review step intentionally — full automation would have introduced trust risk with enterprise clients.

The Outcome

  • → Proposal drafting time: 5 days → 1 day (80% reduction)
  • → 10 live client proposals delivered successfully
  • → Pre-sales team capacity freed up by ~4 hours/proposal
  • → Zero client complaints about proposal quality

What I'd Do Differently

Build a feedback loop from the start — tracking which proposal sections clients responded to most, and using that data to improve the prompt templates over time.
